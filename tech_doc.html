The Pillars of Islam

Islam has five tenets or as we say, five pillars.

The purpose of these tenets is to determine if the individual has truly reach the true status of a Muslim - a submitting person to one God (Allah).

The tenets mentioned are as follows:

  1. Shahadah
  2. Solat
  3. Zakat
  4. Fasting
  5. Hajj

Purifying Oneself

Purification is the most important aspect of the religion. One of the major values the religion of Islam upholds is the focus on the cleanliness.

As practicing Muslims, every religious rituals and practices done should observe cleanliness as the first step to everything.

Purification can be classified into two categories:

  1. Outwardly
  2. Inwardly

In general, outwardly means to ensure cleanliness is observed around your body, clothing and surroundings.

Fundamentals of Prayers

Prayers is the most important subject throughout the course of understanding Islam. Without it, he/she can't be called a true muslim.

Muslims believe that prayers will be the first thing accountable for when we are presented infront of God (Allah)

Prayers are observed daily at stipulaed times. Basically, 5 times a day. The stipulated times are called as follow:

  • Subuh
  • Zuhur
  • Asr
  • Maghrib
  • Isyak

Fundamentals of Zakat

Zakat refers to cleansing ones wealth. Wealth can be classified as:

  • Gold and Silver
  • Jewellery
  • Livestocks
  • Staple Foods:
    1. Seeds
    2. Grains
    3. Fruits
  • Business
  • Investitures
  • Singapore Context - CPF & Insurance

Fundamentals of Fasting

Muslims observe fasting for a full-30-days fasting during the month of Ramadan which is the ninth month in the Hijri calendar just after Syaban.

It is a holy period for the Muslims as they will usually potray the goodness in them and the good deeds will naturally be kept at the highest during this period. We can see many Muslims, despite constraining themselves and depriving from food and drinks for the day, they strive to do good by being good muslims as a basis and to the point of helping others in need!

Indeed this month is truly beautiful and the best time to capture the moments where muslims are basically at their A game. At day, they will fast and they will only break their fast when the sun sets which is during Maghrib. Not only fasting on the day, they will also observe performing prayers called Taraweeh prayers after isyak time and this prayer might last for 2 hours straight.
